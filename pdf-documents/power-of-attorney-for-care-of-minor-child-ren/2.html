1.3.2

CMIA authorization

I am my child’s “patient’s representative” as that term is used in the Confidentiality of Medical Information Act (CMIA), Cal. Health & Safety Code § 123100 et seq. I designate my agent as my child’s personal representative while I am incapacitated. I authorize and direct my health care providers, any health plan, any health care clearinghouse, contractor, or other person or entity with information about my child’s health to give my agent full access to that information, and to treat any request from my agent for that health information as though it came from me.

1.3.2

CMIA authorization

I am my children’s “patient’s representative” as that term is used in the Confidentiality of Medical Information Act (CMIA), Cal. Health & Safety Code § 123100 et seq. I designate my agent as my children’s personal representative while I am incapacitated. I authorize and direct my health care providers, any health plan, any health care clearinghouse, contractor, or other person or entity with information about my children’s health to give my agent full access to that information, and to treat any request from my agent for that health information as though it came from me.

1.4

When agent’s authority becomes effective

My agent’s authority becomes effective upon my incapacitation, provided my child has no other living parent with capacity to consent. My agent, by written declaration under penalty of perjury, may determine conclusively that I am incapacitated.

1.5

When this power of attorney terminates

This power of attorney terminates when I revoke it or when I am no longer incapacitated.
