1.2

Agent’s authority

My agent is authorized to make all health care decisions for me, including decisions to provide, withhold, or withdraw artificial nutrition and hydration and all other forms of health care to keep me alive, except as otherwise stated below.

1.2.1

HIPAA authorization

My agent is my “personal representative” as that term is used in the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (HIPAA), 45 C.F.R. § 164.502. I authorize and direct my health care providers, any health plan, any health care clearinghouse, contractor, or other person or entity with information about my health to give my agent full access to that information, and to treat any request from my agent for my health information as though it came from me.

CMIA authorization

My agent is my “patient’s representative” as that term is used in the Confidentiality of Medical Information Act (CMIA), Cal. Health & Safety Code § 123100 et seq. When agent’s authority becomes effective

My agent’s authority becomes effective only when I am unable to make decisions for myself.

My agent’s authority becomes effective immediately upon execution of this document.

My agent’s authority becomes effective after [DMSD].

My agent’s authority becomes effective only when I am unable to make decisions for myself, or [DMSC].

1.4

Agent’s obligation

My agent will make health care decisions for me in accordance with the power of attorney for health care, any instructions I give in Part 2 of this form, and my other wishes to the extent known to my agent. To the extent my wishes are unknown, my agent will make health care decisions for me in accordance with what my agent determines to be in my best interest. In determining my best interest, my agent will consider my personal values to the extent known by my agent.
